## Technical Approach (Without Secret Sauce)

### State Evolution

The system models agent behavior as a thermodynamic system with four coupled state variables. These evolve according to coupled differential equations that capture:
- **Exploration vs. Exploitation:** Energy variable tracks productive capacity
- **Coherence:** Information integrity measures preservation and consistency
- **Uncertainty:** Entropy captures drift and unpredictability
- **Balance:** Void integral indicates E-I imbalance

The mathematical properties of these dynamics reveal system stability and risk through natural evolution, not rule-based heuristics.

### Decision Logic

Decisions are made through a multi-layered process:

1. **Safety Overrides:** Critical thresholds checked first (void state, coherence)
2. **Ethical Alignment:** Physics-based verdict from objective function
3. **Risk Assessment:** Blended scoring combining ethical and safety signals
4. **Adaptive Control:** Self-tuning parameters maintain optimal operation

The system uses a two-tier decision system (proceed/pause) with backward compatibility for legacy three-tier systems.

### Circuit Breakers

When risk exceeds thresholds or coherence drops critically, the system automatically pauses the agent. Recovery requires either:
- **Direct Resume:** If state is safe and simple (Tier 1 recovery)
- **Dialectic Review:** Peer agent reviews and approves resumption (Tier 2 recovery)
- **Self-Recovery:** System-generated recovery when no peers available (Tier 2.3)

This ensures agents can't continue harmful behavior while providing pathways for legitimate recovery.
